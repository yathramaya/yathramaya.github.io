Live Music Bar at Goreme, Cappadocia, Turkey
Live Music Bar at Goreme, Cappadocia, Turkey

Timeline : 2018 April
Turkey Asia Europe Half Day People Culture City

The cold air was broken by the white trail of warm breath as I walked in the night. The bar was just a block away from where we stayed. The tables were only beginning to get filled up, when I realized that more people were coming in around the same time. We took a table for two, ordered some Raki, that came with some soft bread and dips. The lights were dim and created a warm, cozy atmosphere. Everyone were dressed in partywear, colourful, resplendent with makeup and jewelry. In comparison, I, in my travel attire, managing to salvage the best combination of the eight pieces of clothing I had packed, sat there sticking out like a sore thumb. There was a bar counter in a corner, where a friendly bartender was serving. Several round and oval tables with families, friends, couples spread out in the room. At one end, there was a makeshift stage setup with some speakers and sound systems. We were in for a surprise as we saw that it was not just dinner that awaited us.

Slowly, the lights came on the small stage, a couple of tables away from where we were seated. A guitarist walked on to the stage, took his seat on a bar stool and started tuning his guitar. I was thrilled to know that we came just in time for an evening performance. The whole atmosphere was of celebration, and somehow, even though we did not know the language, I got the feeling that everyone knew eachother and we were in a big party.

It started with a soulful song by the guitarist. He had the most beautiful voice and the Turkish words sounded soothing. It must have been a love song, I guessed. Then came the percussionist and finally, the stage came alive with the female vocalist, who livened up the mood. She had a powerful voice and captivated the audience with the first song itself. The mood only improved, with people in the audience joining along to sing and dance. They all knew the words and they sang with all their heart. A group of friends occupying the table nearest to the stage were completely lost in the signing, I could see that they were enjoying themselves. They used the songs backdrop to express their feelings towards eachother. Another family was celebrating their teenage daughter’s birthday, the cake came along as the performance progressed, the candles were blown and the family celebrated.

Behind us sat a group of four men, in their middle ages, singing the songs and enjoying their beer. How I wish I knew the songs, I wanted to join them and be a part of this live music experience. I smiled at them and they knew that we were not from this place, so they gave warm smiles that made me feel comfortable. The singer then started a song and prompted everyone to join in to complete the lines. Everyone sang the chorus. The percussionist and guitarist kept up with the singer and played exceptionally. The trio did a great job of transforming an everyday dinner experience into a party within minutes. I do remember that the food was great too, but what I distinctly remember about that night was how the music made me feel. Without knowing the tunes, without understanding a word of what was sung, the music touched me, it gave rise to feelings of warmth, of joy, of togetherness and love. The singing reached a crescendo and I sensed that it was about to end, although I wanted to sit there all night listening. The musicians took a break, perhaps, it would go for a little longer into the night. But we had to leave. I could feel the energy for a long time that night. It was a grand ending to our stay in Goreme, Cappadocia.
