Denali at her raw best

Timeline : 2018 August
United States of America Alaska North America Full Day Wildlife Nature

Sitting in the park bus, we felt as though we were on a private tour of Denali, just 4 of us, and our guide cum driver, Nick. By afternoon the entire Denali range was covered by thick clouds, it had started to get dark, our chances of spotting wildlife also diminished with the daylight. With the rain looming on the horizon, we almost had the entire park for ourselves. Driving through thick fog, with our headlights on, with almost no visibility, the raw beauty of Denali, the vast river plains, the mountains showed us yet another side, which was entirely different from what we had seen the previous day. Although the views of Denali mountain range and the wildlife was unparalleled the previous day, I felt a sense of tranquility and adventure together on this trip. It was like a road to nowhere, I was prepared to get lost in this wilderness.

Nick made several stops, a few customary bathroom stops and a few of them to soak in the views. The air outside was chill and initially I was reluctant to step out of the comfort of the bus. But I found myself jumping out compulsively at every stop, taking in the air, enjoying the cool mist on my face. The valleys that looked so bright and green the previous day wore a shade of grey and white, I loved them both. But the solitude this trip offered, the quiteness, the silence of the park, the thunder, these were priceless. The mountains were formidable, the road was trecherous, the rivers were rough, our bus looked like a tiny, fragile lego block slowly moving through the mist. As it started to rain, our chances of spotting any bears completely evaporated. Nick wanted to bring back some excitement to the group and suggested an activity, of hiking a short distance to pick berries, the same berries that the bears are so fond of! So we grabbed our raincoats and jumped out of the bus and started hiking. Nick led us enthusiastically, he pointed us to the berries we could eat and those we shouldn’t eat, which were poisonous, we carefully picked some and tasted them. I was thinking of the bear that might be watching us from a distance wondering who is stealing her berries, like in Goldilocks and the three bears!

Although my shoes were completely soaked (I regretted not wearing boots), my spirits were high. I enjoyed this impromptu activity. Now our trip started to look like fun. Before our next stop, at a place where we had to cross a bridge, Nick suggested we could get off the bus and walk on the bridge, till the viewpoint. It sounded like a great idea, so without a moment of hesitation, I jumped out of the bus and started walking. It was cold. I was wet. But standing over the bridge and watching the river flow, with the rain pouring over it, was something I would cherish. I felt the full force of nature, the raw beauty of Denali, the park in its full glory. There are a few experiences which you would remember, and this bus tour of Denali was definitely one such. By the time we turned back, at the end of our loop, it was dark. We were the only bus in this whole park, without anybody around, except for a few backcountry hikers who might have camped anywhere in the park. This was a unique opportunity that rewarded a few. And I was happy that I could see the park on both the days, one bright and sunny, the other cold, wet and dark. And, without doubt, Denali remains one of my favourites.
