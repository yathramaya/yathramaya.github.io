Turkish Bath at a Hamam, Istanbul
Turkish Bath at a Hamam, Istanbul

Timeline : 2018 April
Turkey Asia Half Day Culture Historical

After having spent two months backpacking and camping, the idea of a nice hot turkish bath seemed most desirable. Although I did not have a first hand experience about a Hamam, having read a few blogs about it, I felt I was bold enough to give it a try. Having been to Istanbul and not trying the Hamam was not acceptable for me. I kept putting it off for the first couple of days, with certain inhibitions taking hold of my cravings of a hot bath. However, one morning, after having a light breakfast, I decided it was time to give in to my aching muscles and let go of my apprehensions.

It was expensive, however, as I walked in, with tensed nerves, the receptionist’s smiling face and friendly voice put me at ease. There were separate sections for men and women. I was asked to choose from three services, the regular bath, where you clean yourself, the assisted bath, where someone bathes you and a third option with an added massage. I chose the third, as the sound of massage was most inviting. The moment she gave me a pair of panties, after looking at my frame, I realized there was no escape. I was here. And bathe I would.

There was whole range of changing rooms on the upper floor, where I saw women in all stages of dressing up or dressing down. I was handed a bath towel and asked to wear only the panties and drape myself in this large towel. I walked down, barefoot, with my hair tied in a bun, and with the towel tied around my body. I was ushered into a pathway with heavy wooden door, leading to the innermost chamber. I could barely breathe as soon as I entered this large dome shaped bath house, because of all the steam. Slowly my eyes got adjusted to the steam and I was led by a sturdy woman, who seemed quite in a hurry. Realizing I did not speak Turkish and this was my first time, she used a combination of sign language and english words to direct me towards the centre heated stone. Here, she asked me to wait, to lie on the heated stone. As soon as I did, I realized it was the most beautiful feeling of warm stone slowly working my muscles and melting away the fatigue. I immediately closed my eyes and drifted off into heavenly sleep. After a few minutes I opened my eyes and saw the huge dome above me, covered with glass, filtering the sunlight, with water droplets, condensed from the evaporated steam, falling on the stone in slow motion. The steam, the heat, the sound of splashing water, everything made it so dreamy.

After few minutes of pure bliss, it was my turn for the bathing. I was not sure what to expect, so I just went along and followed her instructions. In a quick action, she pulled off my towel and laid it on the stone. I was then asked to lie on top of the towel, feeling shy, naked. She smiled and asked me to relax. Then she started splashing warm water on me and scrubbed me with soap and loofah, like never before in my life. I do not recollect even my mother scrubbing me so much in childhood. She was not gently, but she was careful not to hurt me. She applied her strength and in quick strokes scrubbed me like she was polishing a piece of wood. I felt squeaky clean at the end of it. Then she asked me to use any of the open booths along the side of the domed room to wash away the soap and also wash my hair. The water was flowing continuously through antique taps, which felt right out of a period film. Everything was kept as traditional as possible, without ugly plastics in sight. I finished the bath and proceeded to the next step, which was the massage.

I was given a dry towel to wipe myself and then asked to lie on one of the massage table. This time another lady, was ready to give me a massage. The smiling lady asked me where I was from and exclaimed in Turkish and broken english that I had a great body. With this compliment, I lay on the table with my eyes closed looking forward to a massage with aromatic oils. She was firm and gentle, with just the adequate pressure kneading on my aching muscles, that had just become supple from all the scrubbing and hot water. It was deeply invigorating, and I felt a fresh surge of energy. After the massage I had a quick shower and then went to the upstairs area to dry my hair and get dressed. There was no need to rush, everything was relaxed, there was enough room for everyone as it was a huge facility.

Cemberlitas Hamami that I had chosen is one of most ancient, historical hamams in Istanbul. It was renovated to make the infrastructure modern, yet retain the old charm of the architecture, the main dome and the heating system. The staff here were extremely professional. At the end of it, as I walked into the reception area, I had turkish coffee and ready to walk to my room and sink into a well deserved sleep. It was one of the most wonderful experience and something that is unmissable. From that day, for several days I felt squeaky clean, and could not believe the softness. I will not dismiss it now when I hear “you have not had a bath unless you have been to a hamam”. And now, back home, whenever I feel tired and in need of exfoliation, I can only think of this wonderful hamam experience.
